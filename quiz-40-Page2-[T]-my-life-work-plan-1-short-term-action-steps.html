Life-Work Plan 1: Short Term Action Steps

What makes checklist effective is that it allows you to track your progress and feel a sense of accomplishment. Instead of your goal or vision seeming so far away, you can actually see how you are making progress. Having a notebook, journal or app dedicated to your plan is a really good idea.

In the exercise below you’ll create a simple plan with short- and medium-term objectives that should be preparing you for successful transition to the career you are interested in. If you are still in high school, your steps should help you finish high school and earn that diploma or G.E.D. and prepare you for post-high school education. If you have already graduated from high school, ten you’re further along and these objectives should move you closer to a career. They might be financial objectives or designed to help you cover the cost of education or training. They might also help you be better prepared for this career by getting a feeling for what it’s like, developing essential skills, and keeping you motivated. There is a text box for entering your objective and an indented text box below it to break your objective down into at least three action steps (a thing you can do or take action on) though you may have more than 3 action steps. To complete this page and move on you will need to identify at least 8 objectives and their accompanying action steps.

Once you’ve completed at least 8 objectives with their action steps, you can move on if you choose. This is a step you will want to share with your mentor team to get their feedback on your plan. When you click the SAVE button below your answers will create a PDF document titled My Plan: (with the date you created it) and it will be in your completed documents tab on your dashboard. You can share it from there with your mentor team for feedback.

If you want to create a new plan after you have saved one, click the RESET button and start again. This time when you click the SAVE button it will create a new My Plan document dated for the day you created it. You can have up to 5 plans at any one time. If you already have 5 plans, a dialogue box will ask you which one you want to discard to create the new plan. The capacity to create new plans will make this useful beyond the completion of this course. As you complete one set of objectives, you can challenge yourself with a new set. Multiple plans is also a useful way to expand a plan beyond 10 objectives.
