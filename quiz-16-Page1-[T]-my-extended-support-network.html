My Extended Support Network

No one succeeds on their own!

Congratulations! You’ve built a mentor team and learned how to utilize that team effectively. Your mentor team is focused on your transition to adult life and work but there are certainly other areas in your life that are going to present challenges. Since you’ve already begun thinking about a support network for your work-life, which is really smart, you might want to think about who you would consider your extended support network.

What follows is a planner. It isn’t essential that you complete all of it. It’s more to get you thinking about who your go-to people are for support. Use the boxes below to write in names of the people you would turn to for the kinds of help or questions described next to them. This list of your personal support network can and should change over time. Some people might fill multiple slots, but the point is to have more than one person to turn to for help or guidance or advice. If you aren’t sure who to list in a category, that’s okay. Just be aware that it’s easier to think about who you might turn to before you have a crisis. Also, if you share with someone that they are your support network in a specific area, it can strengthen the bond you have with them and give you a strong sense of security and confidence.
