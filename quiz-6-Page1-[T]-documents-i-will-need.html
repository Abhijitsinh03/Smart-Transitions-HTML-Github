Documents I will Need

Essentially, documentation is proof of who you are and important information you will want to have easily accessible. One of the great features of going through this Smart Transitions Online process is that you will be able to scan many if not all of these forms of documentation and upload the to your personal gallery. They will be stored in a cloud server and you can access them from your phone or computer whenever you need them.

If there is one thing that defines adult life, it might well be documentation. It seems like wherever you go and whatever you do, someone will want you to have some kind of documentation, paperwork, or proof of who you are or that what you are saying is true.

Making a smart transition to adult independence means acquiring the appropriate documentation and either having it with you in a wallet or purse or knowing where you can find it when you need it (like accessing it from your Smart Transitions Online Gallery).

In the accompanying checklist, essential items like a driver’s license or photo I.D., social security card, proof of insurance, bank card or credit card, proof of legal status as an immigrant (if necessary) and probably a list of emergency contact information should be with you at all times.

Other items like birth certificates, diplomas or GED Certificates, training certifications and licenses, health records, tax information, or items you won’t need to produce as frequently can be kept in a notebook or a folio with plastic drop in pages to protect the originals. Do yourself a favor now and get organized. Keeping all your documentation in one safe place can mean the difference between getting financial aid or health insurance and being turned down.

For now, it is enough to check off the items you already have and prioritize the things from the “Got To Have” list that you need to acquire first. Review your check list every so often and update it. After you’ve completed this initial task, you will find your documentation check list under the “checklists” tab on your dashboard. If you don’t understand what some of these documents are or how to acquire them, you can do a Google search or ask a parent, a teacher, or a mentor.

Did we forget something?

Okay, but where should I keep these things? If it’s a blue item, you should probably keep it with you in your purse or wallet and don’t leave home without it. If it’s a green item and if you have a car, keep these things in your glove compartment. In an envelope that is easy to find. A traffic stop may go much easier if your registration and proof of insurance are easy to locate.

If it’s a red item, keep it in a safe place, but know where it is. A folder or 3-ring binder with your important papers gathered in one place is a really good idea. Later you will learn how to save these documents to your Smart Transitions Online Gallery so they will always be accessible from your computer or mobile device but for now, just work on getting them together.
