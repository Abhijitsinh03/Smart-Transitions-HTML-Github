Tips for Good Headshots

Processing and Printing Your Portrait

Once you’ve selected the best version of your portrait you might want to save a copy and the use your editing functions on your phone to enhance the portrait. Avoid dramatic or flashy filter effects, but often one of the presets will improve a picture quite nicely.

Ideally, you will want an 8 x 10 sized portrait for your portfolio. You can often get this done right from your cell phone at drug store photo counters. You can also print your photo from an ink jet printer, but as was mentioned previously:

a. Save or export your photos in the largest size possible (or as original). This will allow you to make bigger images that are still sharp and not pixelated.
b. Invest in glossy photo paper from an office supply store. It isn’t expensive and it really makes your images look nice. If you print an 8 x 10 size vertical image on a sheet of 8.5 x 11 photopaper, it will fit nicely into your portfolio.
c. Before you print, make sure your printer dialog box is set for glossy paper and “photo” or the highest quality possible.

Remember that you will want to upload this business professional portrait to your STO Gallery in addition to having a print for your physical portfolio.
