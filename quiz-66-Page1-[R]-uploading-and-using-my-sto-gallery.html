Uploading and Using My STO Gallery

So now you should have at least 4 “Who I Am” Statements in your STO Gallery. If you’ve completed these tasks, you should also have a resume and references sheet in your gallery. In upcoming tasks, you will make sure you have your resume and references ready to go as well as creating a cover sheet and a business/professional headshot.

The next step is to scan and upload the supporting documents that you identified in the last exercise for each of your “Who I Am” statements.

The sample below is how you might begin to organize your portfolio. In a standard presentation book with clear drop-in pages, you put two of your portfolio pieces in each page (one on the front side and one facing toward the back side.

Your portfolio cover page would be back-to-back with your headshot slipped into the same clear page holder. Facing your portrait would be your resume with your references sheet facing out on the back side. When you complete this course you will get a certificate of completion for this program. You might want to save the space opposite your references for this certificate, but that’s up to you.

After that, you will want to begin with one of your “Who I Am” statements. Then insert all the evidence you have (letters of recommendation, photos, awards, certificates, programs, press clippings, inventory sheets, etc.) that supports or backs up that first “Who I Am” statement.

Then you repeat that process until your portfolio is full or you have used all the evidence you have. By the way, it’s okay if your portfolio isn’t full when you start out. It’s a work in progress and it will grow over time. After you have created your first portfolio, it’s fine to reorganize it in different ways as long as it tells your story in a compelling way.
