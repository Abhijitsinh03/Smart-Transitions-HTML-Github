Inviting My Mentors

In the previous task you identified a mentor dream team. Those names and email addresses have automatically been transferred to this page. When you are ready and click “INVITE,” an email invitation will be sent to your mentor team. You can read the email message they will receive at the bottom of this task. If they accept your invitation, we will send them some guidance about being an effective mentor and how to use this platform. Then you will see their initials appear on your mentor team message and chat board. We will cover the topic of how to work with your mentor team in a future reading, but for now, let’s move ahead with inviting.

Option 1—Best Way: Invite your mentor in person (face-to-face or by telephone) first and if they are interested or agree, then click the INVITE button below. This will also be important if you don't yet have an email address for this person. It will also save you time in sending out invitations and waiting for responses. Here’s your pitch: “I’m taking this online course to help me be better prepared for adult life and work. Part of the process is building a small team of mentors who are willing to look over my public documents like my resume, my references, my personal presentation portfolio and my work brand webpage. I’m asking you because I respect you and think you could offer valuable guidance. It won't require a lot of time or personal meetings. It’s all done online so that you can give me feedback and support in a way that is pretty convenient. I hope that my mentors will stick with me, but you can also try it for awhile and see if it’s something you want to do. If you say yes, I just need a good email address for you. You’ll get an invitation by email and if you accept that, they will send you some additional information and guidelines to being a successful mentor. Whatever you decide, thanks for considering my request.”

Option 2—Good Way: If you already have an email address for this person you can copy and paste the request above into an email or text message. The if they say yes you can go ahead and click INVITE by their name on the list below.

Option 3—Way: If you know this person fairly well AND you already have a good email address for them AND you are pretty sure they will agree. You can just go ahead and click “INIVITE.”

Trouble shooting:

If a mentor declines, don't take it personally. People have busy lives and sometimes they are hesitant to take on a new responsibility. Just go back to the task titled “My Mentor Team Planner” and replace the information for the person who declined with a new mentor. That info will automatically show up in this task “Inviting My Mentors” so you can invite someone new.
