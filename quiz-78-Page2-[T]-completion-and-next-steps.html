Completion and Next Steps

First of all, it’s important to remember that this is a journey not a destination. As you’ve gone through this process you’ve built tools that you can use starting wherever you are now and stretching into post-secondary education or skills training. Instead of thinking of this as over and done with, keep these things in mind:

  1. You’ve gathered critical information about yourself into one cloud-based resource that you can access from your mobile device or computer any time you have to fill out a job application. Since you will probably be doing that quite often, keep using this as a resource. Update your information (My Autobiographical Data Form). Use the resume publishing feature to produce updated resumes quickly and easily for work. You will need resumes all the way through college if you pursue that route.
  2. You’ve built a mentor team. This is a powerful resource for when you encounter challenges or need advice. Remember that successful people have support networks. If some of your mentors drop out, replace them with new mentors as you move into work training and/or college.
  3. You’ve built a life-work vision and plan for yourself. The reality is, however, that plans sometimes change. If your plan changes, come back to My Life Work Plan 1 and My Life-Work Plan 2 to update your plans.
  4. You can also use this as a refresher to prepare for interviewing or for reminders of how employers are evaluating your performance.
  5. Finally, one of the most important things you’ve done is create a personal presentation portfolio. This tool can serve you well in interviews and job or special program applications during and after specialized skills training or earing a college degree. Use your STO Gallery to upload new pieces for your portfolio. Replace items that are more than a few years old with more recent evidence.

In other words, you’ve built a powerful resource for yourself here. Keep it up and keep using it.
