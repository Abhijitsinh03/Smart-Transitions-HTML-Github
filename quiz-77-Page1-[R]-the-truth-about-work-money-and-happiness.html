The Truth About Work, Money, and Happiness

You’re nearing the end of this course and you’ve done a lot to position yourself to be successful in the transition to adult life and the workforce. But remember that all along our goal was not simply to help you be successful, but that that success would also make you happy. They are not the same things.

If you are focused on money as a source of happiness, you probably need to understand the following truths about what impacts how much money you are likely to make over your entire lifetime? (In this order)

  1. How much wealth your parents have accumulated. It’s no guarantee of course, and items 2 and 3 can compensate for not coming from a wealthy family, but if your parents make a lot of money, you’re statistically more likely to make a lot of money. That doesn’t seem fair and, we can always find exceptions to the rule, but that’s still the rule.
  2. How much society values your chosen profession. This is just supply and demand. It doesn’t matter how good you are at something. If society doesn’t value it, you won’t make much money doing it. The worst football player in the NFL makes more that the best athlete in many other sports.
  3. Your level of education does make a difference in how much you earn over your lifetime. It isn’t a guarantee and some people get PhDs in things that won’t make them much money unless they teach at the college level, but they have usually picked a subject they love so that happiness can offset the lower salary.
  4. Finally mastery or excellence at your chosen profession does make a difference. How good you are doesn’t always matter as much as how determined you are, but there’s no getting around the need for talent and effort applied toward mastery.

So the lesson here is pick something you like to do that society values and learn to do it really well.
