Letters of Recommendation

Who to Ask:

The people you might ask for a letter of recommendation usually fall into one of four broad categories.

  • Teachers
    • These should be teachers who have had you in class for more than just a few weeks. You are usually looking for a letter that attests to your academic and/or leader¬ship abilities here.
  • Coaches, Mentors, or Activities Sponsors
    • If you have participated in a sport or club, your coach or sponsor might write a letter attesting to your participation and your commitment. If you’ve worked with a mentor, he or she might be willing to attest to your character.
  • Employers
    • If you have been a good employee and left under good circumstances, you can ask for a letter of recommendation confirming your work skills and reliability.
  • Community Leaders
    • If you are active in, youth groups, church groups, or other community activities, or if you do volunteer work, consider getting letters that establish your involvement and responsibility.

Don’t ask parents or family members or peers for letters of recommendation. They will generally not be taken seriously by people reviewing your portfolio. Also, sometimes an employer might reach out to a person writing a letter of recommendation in search of more details, so don't ever fake a letter of recommendation.
