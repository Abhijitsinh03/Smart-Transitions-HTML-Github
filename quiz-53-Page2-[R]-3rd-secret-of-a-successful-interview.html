3rd Secret of a Successful Interview

3rd Secret of a Successful Interview

1. Who you know

Okay, so this may not be fair, but the absolute best way to get hired is to have an inside connection. A recommendation from a friend of the business owner or manager or even a recommendation from a current employee often outweighs other considerations when it comes to hiring. Your friends can be your best resource. Employers don’t always just hire the friends of current employees, but, as an employer, if a good worker recommends someone, that person often gets a chance.

So when you begin looking for a job, ask yourself these questions:

  • Where do my friends work and would they put in a good word for me?
  • Do my parents or family members know any employers with whom they could make a connection for me?
  • Could members of my church or community put in a good word for me with an employer?

Here’s the catch with this strategy. For it to work, two things need to be true. First of all, the friend you are relying on has to have a good reputation with that employer. If he or she doesn’t then that employer is not going to be interested in hiring someone that person recommends. Second, you already have to have a pretty good reputation with your friends and neighbors and you owe it to them to be a really good employee. If someone has recommended you and then you turn out to be a poor employee, you’ve made that person look bad. You’ve ruined your reputation and tarnished theirs. Using friends and family to get a job can be a great strategy, but it also brings with it a big responsibility.

Needless to say, once you are working and someone approaches you about recommending them, be very careful. It’s a good thing to be a friend to someone, but if they get hired on your recommendation and then behave badly on the job it will reflect on you.
