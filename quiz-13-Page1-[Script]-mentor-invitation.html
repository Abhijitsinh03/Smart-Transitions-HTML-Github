Mentor Invitation

Text for email invitation to mentors

(Student name automatically filled in) is registered in the Together We Can Foundation’s Smart Transitions Online Course. This is an extensive self-paced course designed to help youth better prepare for the transition to adult life and work. In addition to learning about adult life and the world of work, they will be assessing their strengths, skills, values and passions. They will also be developing practical tools like a life-work transition plan, a resume, references, a personal presentation portfolio, and a personal brand web page.

Research indicates that one of the most significant things that impacts a youth’s success in the transition to adult life is an effective support network.

(student’s first name) would like you to join his or her online mentor team. She/he has identified you as someone who is:

  • At least 10 years older.
  • With a variety of life experiences.
  • Holding a position of respect in the community.
  • Who is a good listener.
  • Who seems to genuinely care about the welfare of others.
  • Who is a calming and steadying influence.
  • Who has expressed an interest in his or her future and welfare.
  • Someone trustworthy.

If you agree, you will receive some additional instructions from us with guidelines and tips, but in it’s simplest form, this is how it works:

  1. You will be a member of a team so that no one person carries all the responsibility.
  2. You will have a cloud-based dashboard accessible from a computer or mobile device that allows you to follow the work your mentee is doing.
  3. You will be part of a group chat to offer feedback and suggestions. For everyone’s safety and protection, everyone sees what everyone else writes. There are no private conversations or face-to-face meetings. In addition, if your mentee is under 18, one of his or her mentors will be a parent or guardian.
  4. You will receive automated emails when your mentee completes certain projects, earns badges for achievement, or needs specific feedback on a document that will be made public (like a resume or personal brand webpage). This should make it easy to keep up with the process and offer quick and easy encouragement.
