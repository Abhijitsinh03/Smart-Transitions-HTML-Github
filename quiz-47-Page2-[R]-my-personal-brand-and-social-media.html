My Personal Brand and Social Media

  1. Never put into an email or text anything that you would not want the world to hear about, especially when using corporate or business email. Your business email account is not private. Your employer can and often does legally monitor your account. Also, people accidentally forward other people’s confidential emails all the time. They may not mean to, but once it’s done, there is no taking it back.
  2. This is going to be hard to hear, but it’s true. Professional adults don’t manage relationships with texts and emails. The moment a relationship is challenged (turns ugly, becomes combative or mean, seems stuck in a misunderstanding) stop texting and have a real conversation by phone or even better, face-to-face. Conflicts almost never get resolved through texting or emails.

Identify the following statements as true or false based on the reading above:

  1. As long as you have your privacy setting set right, you don't have to worry about your Facebook posts.

  2. My business email account is not private.

  3. Spelling and grammar are not as important as speed in email communication.

  4. Checking email regularly is important even if I prefer that people text me.

  5. You can't get fired for something you post on your personal Facebook or Instagram account.

  6. Conflicts seldom get resolved through texting.
