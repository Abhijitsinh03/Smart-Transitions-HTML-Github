Planning My Portfolio

Your portfolio is going to follow a specific format for now. That format is designed to communicate quickly and clearly what your best qualities are to someone who is glancing through your portfolio. We will do this by first having you create, scan, and upload your portfolio documents to your Smart Transitions Online Gallery. You can then use this gallery to organize your portfolio and publish it digitally, but we will also be stressing the importance of printing out the pages of your portfolio to create a print version to take with you to interviews.

In upcoming tasks, we will help you learn how to create and/or upload the following items which will form the beginning of your portfolio:

A.

Page 1…A Cover Sheet—Your full name on a sheet of paper or cardstock to identify the owner of the portfolio.

B.

Page 2…A Professional Business Headshot—This is a head and shoulders portrait of you in professional attire. You can accomplish this with the help of a friend and the camera on your cell phone.

C.

Page 3…Your Resume—You should already have a resume that you created early in this process, but you may want to circle back and make sure that it has all the information you want to include and looks good. Alternatively, if you have another resume you prefer, you can upload that.

D.

Page 4…Your References—Again, you should already have this created and now you simply need to make sure that it is incorporated in your Smart Transitions Online Gallery.
