Tips for Good Headshots

Cell Phone Portrait Tips

This task is pretty easy as well, but there are some steps involved.

  1. You can do your portrait indoors near a natural light, source like an open window or outdoors in open shade. In either case, try to find a simple background. Words that are readable and busy backgrounds tend to distract from the portrait. A blank wall or a sheet of fabric can make a nice plan backdrop.
  2. If you have friend or a family member who is into photography, you can ask them to help you with a portrait. You can tell them that you need something like the examples above with a simple background isn't distracting.
  3. Use the rear facing camera (have a friend hold the phone and compose the picture). The rear facing camera usually has better resolution which will give you better enlargements.
  4. If your cell phone has a portrait mode, use it. This usually gives you a nicely blurred background and more control over lighting.
  5. Find good light. An open window with daylight streaming through is good. If the light too harsh, you can hang a white sheet in front of the window. Try to avoid using flash from your cell phone camera. It’s seldom flattering. If you are having a portrait professionally done, the photographer may use flash, but he or she will know how to use it correctly, so that’s okay. Also try to avoid artificial light sources that will alter your skin tones in the image. Sit with the light hitting your face from the side so that it creates some shadow on the other side of your face.
  6. Make sure that the camera is physically close enough to you so that your face fills up an imaginary oval that takes up about 2/3 of the picture frame. Make sure the camera is roughly level with your eye line—not looking down at you or looking up at you.
  7. Digital pictures are cheap so take a bunch with subtle variations.
  8. Smile. Remember that the point of this image is to make you seem attractive, personable, and get you hired.

How should I look in my headshot?

A professional business headshot is an opportunity for you to help a potential employer visualize you as the kind of employee they would want to hire, retain, and promote. It's a chance for you to express your work brand in a visual way.
