What is a Work Brand?

Notice how these job seekers are able to turn their qualifications into a description of themselves. It isn't merely a list. It’s statement with examples. These are all strong work brands.

On a final note, you may be wondering if it’s possible to fake a work brand that isn’t really you but just what you think an employer is looking for. The short answer is yes. Some people can convincingly appear to be something they are not, but it is never a good idea. Maintaining a lie (and yes, a faked work brand or set of qualities that are not really who you are is lying) is hard work and stressful. You won’t be able to do it consistently at work and when you stop doing it, your employer is going to wonder what happened to the person they hired. You may not get retained and you certainly won't get promoted. Make your work brand truthful ad authentic.

Identify the following statements as true or false based on the reading above:

  1. Employers, generally, don’t know what they are looking for.

  2. The first cut an employer usually makes is all those people without basic job qualifications.

  3. Of the top 20 success skills employers are looking for, a sense of humor is close to the top of the list.

  4. Faking a work brand is okay if it helps you get hired.

  5. In the examples above, Ashley is really highlighting her teamwork skills.
