Drafting a Life-Work Vision

A Life-Work Vision gives your Life-Work Plan a guiding principal. It helps you make smart choices about your future. Your Life-Work Vision will help you select the career path that best aligns with your aspirations, gifts, skills, passions, and values. Rather than limit you to one specific job, it describes how you want to make a difference in the world. There is a wonderful saying that describes where we would like you to end up:

“Find the place where what the world most needs intersects with the best of your gifts.”

A Life-Work Vision has the following elements:

  1. It begins in the first person-present tense with “I want to…” or “I plan to…” or “I hope to…”
  2. It incorporates your gifts (what you are naturally good at), your skills (abilities you’ve developed or refined), your passions (what you love to do), your values (what matters most to you), and sometimes, your aspirations (what you want out of life).
  3. It describes how you want to spend your most precious resource (your time) by explaining what you want to do and for whom you want to do it.

When you are satisfied with your Life-Work Vision, click on the save button below. It will generate a PDF of your Life-Work Vision that you can access under the Completed Documents tab. Each time you click “save,” it will generate a new numbered version of a Life-Work Vision (up to 5). That way you can use this task to generate several different Life-Work Visions or revise a Life-Work Vision later on. Once you have generated 5 Life-Work Visions, if you click “save,” a dialogue box will ask you which numbered Life Work Vision you want to discard to replace it.
