Part-Time Jobs—Where to Look

Now that you’ve got a sense of how to present yourself at your best by appearing qualified, looking attractive and standing out as memorable in a positive way, it’s time to put those skills to work.

Entry level jobs are usually not glamorous and usually don't pay better than minimum wage, but they do offer invaluable experience on how the world of work works and they do provide experiences to add to your resume.

For entry level jobs the best way to learn about hiring opportunities is by networking with your friends who have jobs. They usually know when their business is hiring or is going to be hiring and if an employer can hire someone without advertising, they probably will. This also gives you an opportunity to use an inside connection if your friends are willing to put in a good word for you.

Beyond word-of-mouth, it used to be that most jobs were advertised in newspapers but now it’s internet Websites like CareerBuilder.com, Indeed.com, Monster.com or Craigslist.org. Since hiring has moved online, it can sometimes be hard to actually get to the interview stage because you get pre-screened through an online application process. Make sure you follow instructions to the letter with online applications and don't leave blanks or provide incomplete information. Don't give them an easy reason to exclude you.

One advantage you have is that after going through this Smart Transitions Online process, you should have all the details you need to fill out any job application either on the resume you created earlier or in the Autobiographical Data Form. Since these things can be accessed from your mobile device, you should never have any reason for not filling out an employment application fully and completely.

But, a follow-up visit to the business with your portfolio in hand to check on your application is a great way to get more face-to-face time with the hiring manager, and shows real motivation.
