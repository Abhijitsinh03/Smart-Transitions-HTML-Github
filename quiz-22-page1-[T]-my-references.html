My References

As a reminder, what you are looking for is 3 or 5 people in the community who would say something positive about you and attest to your character as a person and/or an employee if called by a potential employer.

Also remember that the best place to look for references is among:

  • Teachers with whom you have a good relationship
  • Sports coaches
  • Counselors, case workers, or advisors
  • Pastors and Clergy from your church, mosque, or temple
  • Former employers
  • Youth leaders (Scouting, 4-H, Boys and Girls Clubs, YMCA, YWCA)
  • People in your community who work in a profession you are interested in

The Process (Note: If you think you are not close to the point of actually applying for jobs, you can still use this planner, but don’t ask your refences for their permission until you are closer to actually giving those references out.)

  1. Fill in as much information as you can for five potential references in the spaces below
  2. Ask them each to be a reference. If they say yes, make sure you have the remaining information for each of them. Remember not to beg and to thank them regardless of their answer.
  3. Use the information you gather to complete this planner.
  4. Once it is all filled in, click publish to turn it into a PDF that you can download to print or attach to an email.
