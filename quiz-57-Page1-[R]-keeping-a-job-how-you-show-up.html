Keeping a Job—How You Show Up

Keeping a Job—How You Show Up

How are you showing up?

My Brand: Attractive, Well-Groomed, Polite, Confidant, Energetic

The first and most obvious way you are being evaluated is on your appearance, energy, and manners.

  1. Are you prompt and on time? Nothing says “fire me” like chronically being late to work
  2. Do you dress well? Are your clothes clean and pressed and well cared for? Are they appropriate for your work environment—neither too dressy nor too casual? If you have to wear a uniform, is it clean and pressed? If there is address code for employees, follow it. If not, choose a more classical look rather than something too faddish, too tight, or too extreme (like really high heels). A good rule of thumb is to dress for the job you want, not the job you have. If your male supervisor wears a coat and tie, then, if you’re a man, you should wear a coat and and tie. If you want to be a supervisor, by dressing like one, you make it easy for the people in charge of promotions to think of you as a supervisor.
  3. Are you well groomed? Is your hair clean, cut, and groomed? If you wear make-up it should be a clean and simple daytime look. If you have facial hair is it neatly trimmed? Are your teeth clean and is your breath fresh? Avoid excessive fragrances. Perfume or body spray should not be used in place of showering or bathing.
  4. Are you confident and energetic? Do you psych yourself up before going in to work so that you are cheerful and upbeat? Do you smile easily and often? Do you speak up and not mumble? Do you look people in the eyes and really notice them? Do you have a firm handshake? It’s not a strength contest, but you shouldn’t have a limp-fish handshake.
  5. Are you well mannered? Do you use please and thank you and yes sir and yes ma’am? Do you listen more than speak. Are you unafraid to speak up when there is something that needs to be said?

Identify the following statements as true or false based on the reading above:

  1. If you are scheduled to work at 5:00PM, arriving at 5:07 is okay.

  2. Making sure you look good when you show up for work matters.

  3. Even if you have personal issues that are bothering you, setting them aside before going to work is probably a good idea.
