Experiences I’ve had

Experiences are things you’ve done. While a lot of your education seems to be learning “about” things, there are some things that you just need to experience for yourself, because no amount of learning “about” them will actually prepare you for doing them.

This list could be huge and everyone will have different opinions about what essential experiences might be. Think about the list below as what should be among your highest priorities for experiencing as a young adult.

Keep in mind that there are many wonderful and valuable things to experience in life, and the greater variety of experiences you have the better. But the experiences we have listed below are the kinds of experiences that will best prepare you for adult independence and the world of work.

Nobody is going to have had all of them and your particular list will be unique to you. Some of the things may be a matter of personal taste. That’s why we’ve tried to explain a little bit about what having each experience gives you so that, for example, if team sports are not your thing, is there another way you can develop the teamwork skills that employers value? Between family, school, community, and faith-based programs, most of these experiences are within your reach. How many have you had already? Which experiences could you add to your list?
