What is a resume?

Do I really need a resume?

A lot of entry level jobs don’t require a resume. They ask you to fill out an employment application and that is all they require, but you should still create a resume for yourself for these reasons.

  1. Convenience. Just about everything an employer will ask for in terms of information and dates will already be on your resume, so it makes it easy to fill out your employment application completely and professionally.
  2. Outreach. Having your resume in PDF format means it is easy to attach to emails when doing more generalized job searching using social media. If someone gives you a professional contact’s name and email address, you can easily attach your resume when inquiring about employment opportunities.
  3. Professionalism. Having a professional resume makes you look more polished and professional to an employer. Taking a resume with you to a job interview and providing it to your employer makes you stand out from all the other applicants.
  4. Focus. A resume focuses you on what your strengths actually are. A good resume will evolve and grow and change as you get more and more clear about the job/career you want and the “value proposition” you bring to an employer.
