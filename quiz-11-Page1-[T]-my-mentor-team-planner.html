My Mentor Team Planner

As a reminder, what you are looking for is to build a Smart Transitions support network of at least 4 mentors. You have room for 5, but you will need at least 4 to move forward with the Smart Transitions process.

If you are under 18, you will notice below that one of your mentor slots will need to be filled with a parent or guardian. That is for your protection and even if your parent or guardian chooses to participate minimally, it is really useful to have your parent or guardian see the process you are working through.

If you are enrolled in this course through an organization, they may request that you make one of their counselors, teachers, case workers, mentors, or staff part of your mentor team.

Your mentors will help you be reviewing and commenting on the documents you make public through this process (your resume, your references, your portfolio, and your personal brand URL page). That is why it is important to get you team assembled now.

We can't emphasize enough how important your mentor team (support network) can be to your success. Good mentors are priceless. They are not your peers. They are not your boyfriend or girlfriend. They are honest guides who have been through enough challenging life situations that they can help you when you get stuck, frustrated, or lose hope. Best of all, they will push you when you need to be pushed and hold you to your commitments. Remember that a support network is one of the 4 things everyone needs to be successful.

Also remember that the best place to look for mentors is among:

  1. Teachers with whom you have a good relationship
  2. Sports coaches
  3. Counselors, case workers, or advisors
  4. Pastors and Clergy from your church, mosque, or temple
  5. Former employers
  6. Youth leaders (Scouting, 4-H, Boys and Girls Clubs, YMCA, YWCA)
  7. People in your community who work in a profession you are interested in
  8. Aunts, Uncles, or Grandparents

We will help you with the asking process but first you need to make some choices.

  1. This is just to help you plan. The names and email addresses of the mentors you select will transfer automatically to the next task “Inviting my mentors,” but the request will not be sent until you approve it.
  2. If you need to replace a mentor you can return to this Planner at any time and update your list. You will find it under the tab on your dashboard.
  3. Use the planner below to begin this process. If you have not checked all or most of the boxes under your mentor candidate’s name, you should pause and ask yourself if they would really be the kind of mentor that would help you achieve your dreams.
