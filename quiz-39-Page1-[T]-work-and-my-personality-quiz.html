Work and My Personality

We’ve explored a number of things that go into identifying a career that will be fulfilling and make you happy, but one area that makes a big difference in happiness is how well a job aligns with your personality. This has less to do with a particular aptitude for a kind of work and more to do with what tends to make someone happy.

Read through the questions for each of the groups below and put a check next to ONLY those things that make you really happy when you are doing them. If you've never done them, leave them blank unless you’re sure you’d really enjoy doing it. After completing the questions, we’ll total up the answers and let you know what they mean.

And here are the groups.

C GROUP-Conventional Group:You prefer organizing and processing data in a structured situation and environment. You may have clerical and computational ability. You will tend to avoid ambiguous, free, and unstructured occupations or situations. (bankers, bookkeepers, accountants, secretaries, computer programmers, financial analysts, tax preparers)

A GROUP-Artistic Group: You prefer manipulating physical or intangible materials (ideas) to create art forms or products or performances. You may have artistic, language, or musical abilities) You will avoid structured and repetitive occupations and situations. (actors, composers, musicians, designers, interior decorators, writers, dancers, architects, artists craftspeople, filmmakers, computer game designers)

I GROUP-Investigative Group:You prefer investigating and researching biological, physical, or cultural phenomena. You may have scientific or mathematical ability. You will tend to avoid activities and situations requiring business and enterprise expertise. (geologists, chemists, physicists, mathematicians, laboratory workers, medical technicians, biologists, research doctors, detectives, forensic investigators, park rangers)

R GROUP-Realistic Group:You prefer physical work and working with tools, machines, or animals, usually outdoors. You may have mechanical or athletic ability. You will tend to avoid situations or occupations that involve dealing with people very much. (auto mechanics, aircraft controllers, electricians, plumbers, farmers, fisherman, surveyors, factory workers, carpenters, construction workers, athletes, pilots, some first responders)

S GROUP-Social Group:You prefer working with people to inform, develop, help, or cure. You may have interpersonal and educational abilities. You will tend to avoid situations or occupations that focus on mechanical things or don’t directly involve people. (clinical psychologists, social workers, speech or physical therapists, nurses, doctors, teachers, counselors, religious workers, event planners, social activists, politicians, coaches, some first responders)

E GROUP-Enterprising Group:You will prefer influencing and leading others to achieve organizational goals or economic gains. You may have leadership or persuasive abilities. You will tend to avoid investigative occupations or situations. (entrepreneurs, business executives, executive directors, sales people, travel agents, managers, buyers, real estate sales people)

Take a look at the diagram below. Just as you will be happiest by following a career path that is close to your first or second strongest personality alignment, you should probably avoid the circle that is opposite your strongest circle. If you end up working in that field, you will, most likely, hate your life.
