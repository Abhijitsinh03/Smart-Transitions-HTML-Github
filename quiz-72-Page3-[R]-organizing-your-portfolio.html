Making a Good Impression

One of the first things to consider is how to display your samples in a way that is neat and orderly and makes a good impression.

  • Writing Samples
    • Your writing samples should be typed on clean white paper with your name at the top of the page. Write your samples only if your handwriting is very neat and very legible. If your sample has corrections, comments, and a grade on it, retype it. If this is an original work for your portfolio, have a teacher, parent, or friend check your spelling and punctuation. Remember you are trying to demonstrate that you know how to spell, punctuate, and organize your thoughts in writing. If you are using a slip-in binder like this, staple the pages of each sample together and slip each one into its own plastic page. Most of the time people will glance only at the first page of a multiple page writing sample, so that is all that you need to have facing out. If they want to read more, they can slip the sample out of the plastic page.
  • Letters of Recommendation
    • Each of your letters of recommendation should be displayed in its own plastic page. Three or four letters are enough to include. Too many letters (more than 10) can seem odd.
  • Awards and Certificates
    • Awards and certificates can be displayed in plastic pages. It makes sense to place them opposite pictures and information about the award. If you have a picture of you receiving the award, you might want to include it.
  • Press Clippings
    • The best way to display a press clipping is to cut it out of the newspaper or publication. Be sure to save and include the name of the publication and the date on which the clipping appeared. If necessary, reduce or enlarge the clipping using a good photocopy machine. Arrange the clipping, then tape or paste it onto clean white or black paper the same size as your portfolio pages. This technique will preserve your clipping longer, since newsprint tends to yellow over time. The same technique can be used with programs for performances.
  • Original Artwork
    • Once again, a good way to present original artwork is to use a black and white or color photocopier. This will save wear and tear on your original plan, diagram, sketch, etc. You can also use the reduction setting on the photocopier to make all your artwork a uniform size for your portfolio. If your original artwork is smaller than the plastic pages you are using, another option might be centering and mounting the artwork on black paper and inserting it into your plastic pages.
  • Photographs
    • Some photographs may be self-explanatory, but most could benefit from a caption or brief bit of text describing what the photo is of and why it is in your portfolio. A single photo that is 5x7 can be centered and taped onto a clean piece of white or black construction paper. A caption can be typed and printed out and then cut and pasted onto the page holding the photograph. Smaller photographs might benefit form being grouped together with like photos on the same page (for example, several smaller photos of you at work)
