My Personal Brand and Social Media

Social media includes things like email, texting, Facebook®, Linked-In®, Twitter®, Instagram®, Pinterest®, SnapChat® and other applications and platforms for networking and communicating. Used wisely, these tools can jump-start your career, make connections, and build your brand. Done wrong, they can become a roadblock to employment, advancement, and the life you want.

Here are 6 tips for a more professional brand on social media.

  1. Learn to use email. I know. It isn’t cool. But email is still the standard that most business runs on so get used to it. Unlike texting, where the rules for spelling and grammar are nonexistent, spelling and grammar count in emails. Bad grammar and constant misspellings can make your employer think that you aren't smart and end up passing you over for a promotion or a raise. Read your email over several times slowly and out loud before hitting the send key.
  2. Get a professional email address and use it. Taniquawilliams96@gmail.com is professional. Partygirl166@gmail.com is not. Most likely, Partygirl’s resume and application aren’t ever going to get looked at. Also, check your email regularly. It doesn't matter how professional your email address is if you never check it.
  3. If you have a Facebook® page, clean it up before you start job hunting. Employers often check out potential employees’ Facebook® pages. Scroll all the way through your Facebook® page as if you were a private investigator. What would you learn about you? Are there pictures, posts, links, or group memberships of yours or other people that reflect badly on the brand you are trying to promote? Are there photos of you that are sexually provocative, embarrassing, or in poor taste. Not only do people not get hired for these kinds of posts, but people get fired every day for things that they post or allow to be posted to their pages.
  4. Think your Facebook® posts, texts, Instagram pics and emails are secure and available only to your closest friends? Think again. Even if you think you are the master of your own privacy settings, once you hit post or send, you don’t know where your politically incorrect jokes, alcohol inspired photos, or verbal tirades will end up. It’s just too easy to screen shot or copy and paste images.
