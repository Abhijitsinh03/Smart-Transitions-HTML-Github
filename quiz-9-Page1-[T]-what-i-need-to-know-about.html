What do I need to know about

Knowledge, like life experiences, can be a huge topic and everyone probably has opinions of what someone should know about. For this checklist we have chosen not to focus so much on academic knowledge (things you traditionally learn in school), but rather on things you should know about to increase your likelihood of adult success. Another way of thinking about this is that if you don’t know some of the things below, you could end up suffering from mental or physical health issues, challenges with hunger and shelter, financial and legal problems, etc.

It is probably unreasonable to expect anyone to know everything they might need to know about, but what is more important is to know when a problem is bigger than your capacity to handle it on your own and to know where you could turn to ask for help with that problem.

Knowledge about the external world is important and some of the items on our list reflect that. But self-knowledge is equally important and is often overlooked in the academic push to pass tests and graduate. With the list below, only check the box if you are fairly confident that:

  1. You know when a problem is bigger than something you can handle on your own, and;
  2. You know where to turn or who to ask for help.
