My Job Planner

In drafting your Life-Work Vision, you probably came up with a career field that would be a good fit for letting you make your vision a reality. A career field is a general area like business, health, medicine, law enforcement, sports, entertainment, engineering, architecture, art, journalism, politics, education, science, military service, law, banking and finance, mechanics, social or human services, technology, food service, hospitality etc.

But there are often multiple jobs with different education and training requirements within each career field. You could work in the medical field with a short-term certification program (medical assistant), an associates or bachelors degree equivalent (nurse or nurse practitioner) or with a bachelors degree plus 4-year medical school plus residency training (doctor).

In order to give you choices and options, use the planner below to first list your career field. If you’ve identified more than one career field, that’s fine too.

My Career Field is:

Next list all the jobs you can think of or have heard of that would let you live your Life-Work Vision. Every job requires training, skills, and knowledge. Consider how most people who have that job acquire that training. For example, waiters and waitresses learn with on-the-job training. Plumbers and electricians usually apprentice. Computer repair people take certification courses. Teachers get Bachelors Degrees at colleges. What kind of training will you need? Beside each job put a check next to the amount or kind of training needed for that job.

So what if you don’t know what the training requirement is for a particular job of career? First of all, try to fill in at least one job and take a guess at what the education or training requirement is. After that, you can move on and come back to this as a planner. Remember, it helps you to have more options, but if you want to be even better prepared, you can do two things. After filling in at least one job possibility above you can move onto the next reading about the educational path for different types of careers and then come back to this My Job Planner to complete it.

Or you can use the internet to do a little career research. We suggest The Bureau of Labor Statistics website (www.bls.gov/ooh/). By clicking on a career field under Occupation Groups on the left side of the page, you can find jobs that fall under that career field with a job summary, the entry level education requirement and median pay. By then clicking on the job itself, you can get more detailed information.
