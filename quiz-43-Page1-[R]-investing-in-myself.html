Investing in Myself

Investment: Something you do or pay for now in anticipation of greater financial reward later.

The best investment you can make for your future is education and training now.

These figures are for 2019 so you can expect that the current numbers are a bit higher, but this should give you an idea of the value of the education and training you get now.

Keep in mind that these are median or average weekly and yearly salaries. Some people make less and some people make significantly more. The point to note here is that for each level up you go in education, there is a rather significant jump in income.

Level of Education Average Weekly Income Average Yearly Income
Some High School No Diploma of G.E.D. $320 $16,640**
High School Graduate $746 $38,792
Some college, no degree $833 $43,316
Technical or Vocational Training * $855 $44,460
Associate’s Degree (2 yr) $887 $46,124
Bachelor’s Degree (4 yr) $1,248 $64,896
Master’s Degree (6 yr+) $1,497 $77,844
Doctoral Degree $1,883 $97,916
Professional Degree Dr./Lawyer $1,861 $96,772

*Technical or vocational training is often hard to quantify because average pay in some fields is very different than other fields. Health care workers tend to make more than HVAC workers, for instance. There is also a big difference between working for someone as a plumber or electrician, for instance, and owning your own plumbing or electrical business. If money is what matters most to you make sure you do some research on your vocational or technical training at the Bureau of Labor Statistics www.bls.gov/ooh/. This will help you identify not just median salary but also starting or entry level salary.

**The government considers anything under $12,760 in annual income to be living in poverty. With no high school diploma and only a minimum wage job, you would barely be above the poverty level.

This is not meant to depress you, but, rather to inspire you to stay in school or get that certification or degree. The further away you get from school or training, the harder it is to make time to go back. Invest in yourself now, while you’re young. It will pay off.

Identify the following statements as true or false based on the reading above:

  1. It is impossible to invest in yourself.

  2. If you are willing to work, having a degree or a certificate doesn't make any difference to how much money you can make.

  3. Working for someone vs. working for yourself can make a difference in your income level.

  4. Median or average income is the same as starting or entry level income.

  5. On average two years of college with an Associate’s Degree earns you about $7,332 more per year.

  6. If you make $12,500 per year even though you are working full time, you are living in poverty.
