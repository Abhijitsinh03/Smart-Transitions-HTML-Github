Careers I Might Like

We’ve been talking about resumes and helping you build your own resume, but it might be helpful to note that resumes tend to be more applicable to careers than to simple jobs to earn money. Many part-time or entry level jobs just require a job-specific application, but having a complete resume will save you time (because you have all the information they will ask you for on that application at hand in your resume) and it will make you appear more professional and competent.

A job is a place where you work. They pay you wages based on the hours you work or they pay you an annual salary divided up by the number of weeks or months in a year. Sometimes they also provide benefits like health insurance or contributing to your retirement fund. You’ll have many jobs in your life.

A career is an occupation undertaken for a significant period of a person’s life and with opportunities for progress. Ideally a career is built around things you love to do, things you are good at doing, and things that make a positive difference in the world.

While it is never too early to begin building a career, most young people begin working by finding a job to earn their own money. Also the same skills required to find a job hold true whether that job is just a way to earn money or whether it is part of a career plan.

Back in the task titled My Smart Start for Success (which you can review by looking under the tab “My Documents”), you identified a career that was interesting to you. Now we are going to take this a step further. A smart career choice is one that aligns with who you really are. It utilizes your gifts and skills, it is work you enjoy and are even passionate about, it matches your values and it brings you the kind of life you desire. We will be exploring those things in the next few tasks but for now we’d like you to identify three careers that you have an interest in and what the path to that career is. It’s okay of one of these is the one you identified earlier.
