What is a Personal Brand?

What is a Personal Brand?

(Who knows, maybe, by the time you see this we will be back to shaking hands again, post Coronavirus.)

Your brand is one of your strongest assets. There are three steps to a strong brand:

Defining: To define your brand you have to know yourself. You have to know what your strengths and talents truly are. While you can certainly build your brand by adopting new productive and attractive traits, faking a brand never works for long. Fortunately, you’ve already been working on this in earlier exercises where you inventoried your gifts, skills, passions, and values. Now you need to condense, simplify and package that brand.

Cultivating: To cultivate your brand you need to start practicing presenting your “brand” traits and qualities to the world now. If you wait until you think someone is watching, it will be too late. You need to eat, breathe, and live your brand 24/7. The biggest mistake people make with a brand is to think of it as fake version of themselves they whip out when necessary to fool people. This never works for long. Another mistake is trying to have too many different brands and pleasing too many people.

Protecting: You protect your brand by asking a simple question when you are about to make a decision—Will this action build my brand or hurt it? If it will hurt your brand and you do it anyway, that’s okay. It just means you aren’t thinking and acting like an adult yet. For example, if a key part of your brand is that you are honest and trustworthy, what are the possible consequences of lying to a friend? If an important element of your brand is reliability, what kind of impact would it have on your brand to blow off commitments?

In our next task we’ll get more specific about what your personal brand is. Then, later we will develop a variation of your personal brand that we call a work brand. For now, though, just answer these questions. You’ve got this.

Identify the following statements as true or false based on the reading above:

The Dallas Cowboys cultivate the image and personal brand of being America’s Football Team.

The more personal brands I have, the better.

If I’m not paying attention to what my brand is, then I probably don’t have a brand.

My brand should be the best me I have to offer.

Faking a brand is a good way to impress people in the long run.

I protect my brand by not behaving in ways that undercut it.
