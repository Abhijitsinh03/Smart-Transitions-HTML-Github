1st Secret of a Successful Interview

The other thing to remember is that what we’re really talking about is your future.

As a teenager or young adult your brain isn’t fully wired to think about the future or future consequences, but the more adult you become, the more you will be considering your future. The ability to find a job is a life skill. That means you are going to need it over and over again for your entire life. If you are entering the workforce today, current research suggests that you will have between 12 and 15 different jobs over the course of your life. Getting good at getting a job just makes sense.

So why not begin developing those tools (resumes and portfolios) and practicing those skills (personal presentation, networking, and interviewing) now? If you blow a couple of fast food job interviews when you’re just starting out, but learn from your mistakes, you’ll be that much better prepared when you’re applying for a job that really matters to you.

So the bottom line is that the interview is your last best chance of getting hired and every interview has three components—3 secrets to success. Secret 2 is appearing attractive. Secret 3 is making yourself stand out and be memorable in a positive way. We’ll focus on those next, but the first secret is appearing qualified. That means walking into an interview with strong sense of your best qualities and a well-rehearsed plan for how you are going to communicate that.

If someone offered you $1000 for every “widget” you could sell, what would your first question be? It would (or should) be “What’s a widget?” If you don't know what you are selling, how successful do you think you’ll be? Well, in a job interview, the widget is you. You need to know exactly who you are and what you are offering. You are selling yourself as an employee to an employer. Your qualifications define you.

Identify the following statements as true or false based on the reading above:

You don't need to be prepared for an interview, just answer the questions you are asked.

A strong economy and low unemployment will make your job search harder.

Your first goal should be to walk into an interview with strong sense of your best qualities and a well-rehearsed plan for how you are going to communicate that.

Success skills even make a difference in jobs that require specific job or technical skills and training.
