Life-Work Plan 1: Short Term Action Steps

Remember that successful people have realistic plans, even when those plans lead to big dreams. Part of what makes plans realistic is that they are made up of small action steps. Action steps are actionable items—things you can do, take an action on, complete, and check off your list. Action steps are really important because often our visions (our long-term goal or objective) can feel very far away almost like the top of a mountain. It can seem overwhelming at first. But remember that you climb a mountain by taking one step at a time.

Your action steps should be in the form of a checklist. When thinking about your action steps, you may find that an action can be broken down into even smaller action items. That’s a good thing. It makes a task even more manageable. For example maybe an action step for you is get some experience that will help prepare you for a health care career while still in high school. But that action step can be broken down further based on the experiences you identify. Perhaps first aid training would look good on your resume. So now your next action step might be to get first aid training, but that can be broken down even further. Your action steps might look like this:

  1. Get some experiences that would prepare you for a health care career.
    2. a.

    Get First Aid Training

    b.

    Volunteer at hospital or Senior Living Center

    c.

    Take a vocational or technical training course in a health care related field through your high school.
