Autobiographical Data Form

This is your autobiographical data form. It is the one of the most important parts of this process. It will gather in one convenient place a lot of the information you will need to apply for jobs in the future. It will be stored securely on a cloud-based server and you can access it from your computer or mobile device. It will also be the source for your resume information. Once you have completed this form, your resume will be available at the touch of a button. And, when you want to edit or add to your resume, you will return to this page. Take your time and work through this form answering the questions as completely as you can. If you need to take a break and return to the form later, you can find it by clicking on the “My Life Channel.”

Awards

Awards are something you earn for outstanding or exemplary performance, skill, participation, or engagement. You can earn them for sports and athletics, academics, drama, art, music, volunteer work, attendance, and citizenship, among other things. Awards can be a significant addition to your portfolio and resume. If you’ve earned them, you should share that fact. If you don’t know where your awards are, check with your parents. They tend to save those kinds of things. Most awards early in our lives have a limited life of two to four years. That means that by the time you are a senior in high school, an award you received in grade school or even middle school is less significant. Your most important awards, with some exceptions are your most recent ones. But it never hurts to scan and upload those earlier awards to your gallery so that you have easy access to them if you want to use them. It is also true that awards for outstanding performance are generally more impressive than awards for participation.

Section 5. Not required to be complete before creating resume

Employment

Your employment or volunteer experiences are the core of your resume. If you haven’t had either, that’s okay, but when you do have them, make sure to come back here and add the experience.

What counts? Working as a volunteer counts (even court-assigned community service can be considered volunteer work, you would just leave off the part about it being court-assigned). Unpaid internships are also a kind of volunteer work. Self-employment counts. Have you been paid to provide babysitting service or grass cutting? If so, you can list those things. And, of course, paid employment always counts. If you have had any of these experiences, list each of them and all of them (by clicking on the Add an Additional Work Experience Button). If you have not had any work experiences you can skip this section.

The work history information on your resume is also what you will need to provide on most job application forms. If you take the time to gather that information now, it will always be available to you via your computer or mobile device and it will take some of the stress out of filling out those job application forms. If you have held many jobs for a very short period of time, that can detract from the image you may want to project. It can look like you either quit a lot (were unreliable) or were fired a lot (were a difficult employee). You might want to only list the several jobs you held for the longest period or that you know would provide the best recommendation for you.

Section 7. Required to be complete before creating resume

Basic Employability Soft Skills

The soft skills that employers look for in basic and advanced level employees are remarkably consistent. Soft skills have more to do with things like your attitude, your work ethic, and your character. We will be exploring these things and helping you refine your understanding of your personal strengths in greater depth in future readings and tasks, but for now, we’ve assembled a list of some of the top things employers look for. Initially we are going to ask you to select the top three statements that best and most honestly define your employability skills or qualities. Remember that it is really important that you are honest about these skills because an employer is going to expect you to back up your claims with actual performance every time you show up to work. If you are just picking things that you think an employer wants to hear, you will not be able to maintain them for long. In the end, it will damage your credibility and integrity at work. Remember that if you pick an item to be listed on your resume, an employer may ask you for an example from your life experience that demonstrates this quality in action.

Remember that your hard skills and your soft skills may change over time. To reflect that on your resume, you will want to return to your Autobiographical Data Form and update it.
