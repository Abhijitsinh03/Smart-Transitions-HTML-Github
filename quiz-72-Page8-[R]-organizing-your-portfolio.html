A Good Portfolio Is a Living Document

That means that when you have finished assembling it, you have marked a moment in time. You have done the hard part. You have assembled your evidence and turned it into a dynamic presentation. As it is, it’s good for about three months without further attention. After that you need to do some updating. You probably have had some interesting and noteworthy experiences in that time. You may have some new skills, and your existing skills have probably improved.

Now that you know what you will need for your portfolio, you should be documenting your achievements on a regular basis with an eye toward including them. Remember to save those good writing samples. Photograph your projects and performances. Collect and value all the certificates and awards you can. Ask for new letters of recommendation. Meet with a favorite teacher on a regular basis to review your portfolio. Share it with your parents. Replace old, sloppy, or worn-out pages with new ones. If your portfolio container looks ragged, replace it. Remember, in the eyes of someone looking at it, you may be only as good as your portfolio.

Be proud of your portfolio and be proud of yourself.
