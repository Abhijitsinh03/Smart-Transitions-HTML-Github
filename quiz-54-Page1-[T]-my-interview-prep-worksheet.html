My Interview Prep Worksheet

A successful interview is a performance and, like any performance or sporting event you wouldn’t think about going into it unprepared would you?

Well, the good news is that after getting this far in this course, you’ve already done a lot of preparing. You’ve got answers to a lot of the questions you will get asked in your interview.

From your Stacking the D.E.C.K. exercise early on, you know what kinds of certificates and documents you may need to produce. You know some of the key life experiences you’ve had. You also know some of the things you have learned how to do.

You’ve built a mentor team who can give you specific advice for particular job interviews.

You’ve created a resume. Remember that if you haven't completed your resume yet or you need to modify it, you can always return to the task titled “My Autobiographical Data Form,” from there you can generate a new Resume in PDF form. You can send this digitally or print it out and take a clean copy with you to the interview.

You’ve gone through a significant process of identifying your life aspirations, your gifts and talents, your skills, the things you are passionate about and your values. These things should be what you talk about in an interview. It might be good to refresh your memory of these things by returning to these tasks or completed documents.

You have a life-work vision and a life-work plan. It’s difficult to underestimate how impressive it is to an employer when a young person can speak of their life pan and goals, even when the job they are applying for is a food service job or retail.

You’ve also identified a personal brand and a work brand and are able to speak to your best qualities and qualifications as an employee.

Finally, you know the inside secrets for successful interviews: Being qualified, attractive, and memorable.

Now we are going to pull those things together into a worksheet to prepare you for an interview. You can use this process in two ways. The first is to fill out the boxes below with as much information or notes to yourself as you can for a specific interview, When you are done, you can publish it as a document and print it out to review before the interview. You can produce up to five different interview preps sheets by editing this document and publishing it. After you have five it will ask you to delete one before saving another.

Alternatively, you can print out a blank version of this planner and fil it out by hand.

The employer I’m interviewing with (ex. McDonalds)

The position I’m applying for (ex. cashier, cook, whatever they have)

The date and time of the interview

The person I’m interviewing with (his or her name and position)

Do I have a connection recommending me and if so whom?

What is this business known for (its selling points or what it advertises)?

What qualities do I think the employer is looking for?

My four best qualifications for this job

An example to support qualification #1

An example to support qualification #2

An example to support qualification #3

An example to support qualification #4

My answer to why you should hire me

Once you’ve completed the worksheet, when you click the SAVE button below your answers will create a PDF document titled My Interview #(1-5) and it will be in your completed documents tab on your dashboard. You can share it from there with your mentor team for feedback.

If you want to create a new interview prep sheet after you have saved one, click the RESET button and start again. This time when you click the SAVE button it will create a new My Interview #(1-5) document. You can have up to 5 prep sheets at any one time. If you already have 5, a dialogue box will ask you which one you want to discard to create the new prep sheet. The capacity to create new prep sheets will make this useful beyond the completion of this course. By clicking the Blank Interview Prep Sheet button below, it will create a blank version of this document that you can print out and fill out by hand.

NOTE: If you are not ready to begin interviewing a this time, just click on the Blank Interview Prep Sheet button below to save a blank version and then you can move on. When you are ready to interview, this can be a valuable tool so don't forget to come back to this task in the future.
