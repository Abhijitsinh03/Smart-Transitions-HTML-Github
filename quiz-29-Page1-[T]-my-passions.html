My Passions

This task should be easy. At least we hope it is. What we are looking for here is what you love to do, how you love to spend your time, what you most enjoy. These are the things for which we are said to have a passion. You don’t need to be good at this particular thing as long as it gives you pleasure, though working to get better at a passion is often extremely rewarding. Engaging in a passion tends to release dopamine and serotonin in your body that you experience as pleasure and enjoyment.

How do you know if something is a passion for you. First of all, it will probably be something you choose to do as often as you can and that you never or seldom see as a burden. Second, it will be something in which you lose track of time. You move into “the zone” or what is called a flow state where you stop paying attention to the outside world and often lose track of how much time has passed. Often passions have a physical or creative component to them like yoga, skateboarding, traditional or extreme sports or music, writing, art, or dance. Sometimes passions have an exploratory element like reading a new book, wandering in a new landscape, or engaging a new culture or country. And, sometimes passions can involve collecting things like shoes, butterflies, stamps, or rare coins.
