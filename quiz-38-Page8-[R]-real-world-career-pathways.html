Entrepreneurial Careerss

All entrepreneurs are at some point small business owners, but not all small business owners are entrepreneurs. If you want to open a hot dog stand, you are small business owner. If you want to see your signature hot dog stands in every city of the world, you’re an entrepreneur. If you want to open a used video game store, you’re a small business owner. If you want to invent a radical new way for people from around the world to recycle and share old video games, you’re probably an entrepreneur.

Bill Gates, who started Microsoft Computer has been paraphrased as saying that the best way to become an entrepreneur is to study how great start-up businesses succeed and then put that into practice by starting a business. If you have a great idea or product that you are passionate about, the entrepreneurial path would be to seek out the specific training, advice, and education you need to turn your idea into a business and then dive in. There are probably organizations in your area that nurture and support entrepreneurial start-up businesses. But remember, like artistic and athletic careers, this path requires talent, luck, great planning and exceptional self-discipline.

We hope this has helped you better understand the career paths for jobs you might have identified. For more specific detail, we suggest visiting The Bureau of Labor Statistics website ( www.bls.gov/ooh/). By clicking on a career field under Occupation Groups on the left side of the page, you can find jobs that fall under that career field with a job summary, the entry level education requirement and median pay. By then clicking on the job itself, you can get more detailed information.
